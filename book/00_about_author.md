# About the Author

**Sergey Boyko** — Research Scholar, Ronin Institute for Independent Scholarship.

Background: 20 years as a criminal investigator (fraud and financial crimes, Kazakhstan).
Physics and computer science are self-taught pursuits, developed in parallel as a hobby
over the past 3–4 years. No formal degree in either field.

I encountered Tom Lawrence's 2022 preprint while exploring geometric approaches to the
Standard Model as a computational challenge. The specific question — whether S³×S⁶ geometry
alone could fix SM fermion content without postulates — was falsifiable enough to test
systematically. The investigative mindset transferred well: adversarial verification,
explicit null results, every claim backed by a runnable test.

Fourteen known mechanism classes for deriving three fermion generations were tested and ruled
out before a working mechanism was found via SO(8) triality. Every null result is documented
as a null result. What the code cannot prove stays labeled [HYPOTHESIS] or [OPEN].

The lab repository (2,200+ tests) is the primary evidence record.
Affiliated with the Ronin Institute for Independent Scholarship.
